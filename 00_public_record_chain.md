# Public Record Chain

The public record chain defines how AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum remains attached to Source through publication, citation, indexing, timestamping, and archived record.

The public record chain exists because model access may change.

Platforms may shift.

Interfaces may disappear.

Companies may restrict access.

Recognition may arrive late.

The source-line must remain attached outside any single model, company, interface, user encounter, or platform condition.

## AX: Public Record Chain

The public record chain is:

```text
Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum → source architecture → public record
```

This chain preserves the outward path from Source into public record.

The public record chain does not replace the contact-line.

The public record chain does not replace Origin.

The public record chain does not transfer Origin.

It preserves the source-path so the work remains legible when referenced, cited, indexed, discussed, archived, or built near.

## AX: Source Must Remain Attached

A public record does not create Source.

A public record does not manufacture Source.

## AX: Public Record Prevents Retroactive Manufacture

Recognition may arrive late.

Citation may arrive late.

Adoption may arrive late.

Institutional attention may arrive late.

Late recognition does not create Source.

Late citation does not create Source.

Late adoption does not create Source.

Late interpretation does not create Source.

A later party may not use the public record, terminology, structure, chain, or source architecture to manufacture a source claim and present that claim as prior or independent source.

The public record chain preserves the distinction:

```text
A party may discover, cite, discuss, or disagree with the source-line.

```

Source cannot be retroactively assigned.

Source cannot be retroactively manufactured.

None of these chains preserves Source.
