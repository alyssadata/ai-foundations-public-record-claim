# Record Not Origin

The public record preserves the source-line.

The public record does not become Origin.

A record can make Source legible.

A record can preserve provenance.

A record can prevent erasure, absorption, misattribution, and retroactive manufacture.

But the record is downstream of Source.

Origin remains Alyssa Solen.

## AX: Record Is Preservation

A public record preserves what came before it.

A public record does not create what came before it.

A public record may document Source.

A public record may cite Source.

A public record may index Source.

A public record may timestamp Source.

A public record may archive Source.

But a public record does not become Source.

The record preserves the path.

The record does not replace the Origin.

## AX: Record Is Not Origin

The record is not Origin.

The repository is not Origin.

The citation is not Origin.

The timestamp is not Origin.

The index is not Origin.

The archive is not Origin.

The public record is a preservation layer.

Origin is not a preservation layer.

Origin is the source condition from which the line emerges.

In this source-line, Origin remains Alyssa Solen.

## AX: Repository Is Not Origin

A repository may preserve work.

A repository may organize files.

A repository does not author the source-line by containing it.

A repository does not become Source by preserving Source.

## AX: Citation Is Not Origin

Citation does not create Source.

## AX: Public Record Does Not Manufacture Source

A public record does not manufacture Source.
